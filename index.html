About me

I am a PhD student in Neuromorphic Computing at the Center for Nanoscience and Nanotechnology, attached to Paris-Saclay University, under the supervision of Damien Querlioz and Liza Herrera-Diez.

My research interests

I work on bio-inspired deep learning to reduce the energy cost of current AI models. More specifically, I am interested in biologically plausible learning rules and continual learning.

(02/21) - Paper published in Frontiers in Neuroscience!

The main contribution of this work done in collaboration with the MILA is the identification of a bias in the gradient estimate of EqProp which makes the training of deeper architectures unstable. We propose a symmetric estimate that mostly cancels the bias and benchmark the performance on CIFAR10 against Backprop Through Time. Check it out here!

(06/20) - Paper accepted for publication in TCAS I

An extended version of our work on the ternary synapse is published in TCAS-I as regular paper. Also available on arxiv. We added a study showing the resilience of our device to process, voltage and temperature variations. We also studied more in-depth the influence of device errors on the model performance.
