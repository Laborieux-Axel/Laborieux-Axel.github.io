About me

I am a PhD student in Neuromorphic Computing at the Center for Nanoscience and Nanotechnology, attached to Paris-Saclay University, under the supervision of Damien Querlioz and Liza Herrera-Diez. I work on bio-inspired deep learning to reduce the energy cost of current AI models. More specifically, my work will help design low-power hardware dedicated to AI (inference and learning).

News

(02/21) - Paper published in Frontiers in Neuroscience!

The main contribution of this work done in collaboration with the MILA is the identification of a bias in the gradient estimate of EqProp which makes the training of deeper architectures unstable. We propose a symmetric estimate that mostly cancels the bias and benchmark the performance on CIFAR10 against Backprop Through Time. Check it out here!

(01/21) - Poster at Cosyne 2021

Our abstract on synaptic metaplasticity in binarized neural networks has been accepted as a poster at Cosyne 2021.

(11/20) - Poster at a NeurIPS 2020 workshop

Our abstract on scaling EqProp has been accepted as a poster at the NeurIPS workshop Beyond Backpropagation. I also made a video presentation.

(09/20) - Our paper got an award at AICAS2020!

We got the First honorary mention which is equivalent to the 2nd best paper award out of the 73 papers presented at the conference. I cannot be thankful enough to my amazing co-authors and supervisors Damien Querlioz and Liza Herrera-Diez!

(06/20) - Paper accepted for publication in TCAS I

An extended version of our work on the ternary synapse is published in TCAS-I as regular paper. Also available on arxiv. We added a study showing the resilience of our device to process, voltage and temperature variations. We also studied more in-depth the influence of device errors on the model performance.

(12/19) - Paper accepted for a Lecture at AICAS2020

Our work on the implementation of a ternary neural network using RRAMs is in the proceedings of the AICAS2020 conference. This is done in collaboration with the IM2NP and CEA-Leti. The paper is available here and on arxiv. You can find the slides of the lecture I gave here.
