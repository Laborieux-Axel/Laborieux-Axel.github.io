About me

I am a PhD student in Neuromorphic Computing at the Center for Nanoscience and Nanotechnology, attached to Paris-Saclay University. I work on bio-inspired deep learning to reduce the energy cost of current AI models. More specifically, my work will help design low-power hardware dedicated to AI (inference and learning).

News

(02/21) - Paper published in Frontiers in Neuroscience!

The main contribution of this work done in collaboration with the MILA is the identification of a bias in the gradient estimate of EqProp which makes the training of deeper architectures unstable. We propose a symmetric estimate that mostly cancels the bias and benchmark the performance on CIFAR10 against Backprop Through Time. Check it out here!
